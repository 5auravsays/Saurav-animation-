Fragments

I

The sea keeps every letter
it was never sent —
folded into salt,
addressed to no one,
read by everyone.

II

Somewhere past the light,
a slow blue animal
carries the shape
of every idea
too large to hold.

III

We are built of the same
thing as the tide —
arriving, leaving,
arriving again,
never once the same water.

IV

Stars are just the ocean
learning to be quiet.
Look up. Look down.
It was always
one water.
